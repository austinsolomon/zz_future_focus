## Pro Tips

### If you don't know exact numbers:
Use ranges:
- Team size: "5-7 people"
- Time split: "mostly execution, some analysis"
- Top time-sinks: "Reporting takes up most of Friday"

Claude will estimate and note assumptions.

### If you want to focus on specific pain points:
Add at the end:
```
Prioritize workflows that:
- Reduce response time to customers
- Eliminate manual data entry
- Improve personalization at scale
- Free up time for strategic work
```

### If you have compliance/security constraints:
Add at the end:
```
Constraints:
- Must comply with SOC 2 data handling
- Cannot auto-send external communications
- Must log all AI decisions for audit
- PII must stay within approved systems
```

## Multi-Department Discovery

To discover workflows across multiple departments in one session:

```
Apply the Workflow Discovery Framework to these 3 functions at our B2B SaaS company:

**1. BDR Team**
- Team size: 8 BDRs
- Daily volume: 30 inbound + 50 outbound per person
- Tools: Salesforce, Outreach, ZoomInfo, Marketo
- Top time-sink: Lead research (10min/lead × 30/day)

**2. Marketing Team**
- Team size: 10 people
- Weekly output: 5 campaigns, 10 content pieces
- Tools: Marketo, Salesforce, WordPress, Google Analytics
- Top time-sink: Campaign reporting (6hrs/week per person)

**3. Customer Success**
- Team size: 12 CSMs, 40 accounts each
- Quarterly load: 120 QBRs
- Tools: Gainsight, Salesforce, Zendesk, Looker
- Top time-sink: QBR prep (4hrs per account)

For each function, generate:
- Top 5 automation candidates (15 total)
- Cross-functional workflow opportunities (e.g., Marketing → BDR handoff)
- ROI ranking across all 15+ candidates
- Suggested build sequence (dependencies between workflows)
```

This will identify both department-specific and cross-functional automation opportunities.
